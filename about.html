About Abisko National Park


Where is this protected area located?

Abisko National Park is in Norrbotten County, Sweden, near the Norwegian border and within the Artic Circle.

What borders this protected area?

The park is bordered by Tornetrask Lake to the east and mountanious terrain on other sides.

What types of activities are allowed?

Recreational activities such as hiking, birdwatching, and northern lights viewing are encouraged in Abisko National Park. Visitors are welcome to gather edible mushrooms and berries, and motorboat travel is permitted on Tornetrask Lake. Dogs are allowed from January 1 to April 30 if leashed, and year-round on roads, marked trails, and near the Abisko Tourist Station.

However, to protect the park's natural environment, certain activities are restricted. Camping is only allowed at designated campsites and fires may only be made in specified areas (outdoor stoves are permitted, but grills are not). Fishing, hunting, and disturbing wildlife are prohibited, as is damaging plants, trees, or natural features. Additionally, drones and motor vehicles are restricted to designated areas, and the Abeskoeatnus delta bird sanctuary is closed from May 1 to July 31 to protect nesting birds. Disturbing grazing reindeer or interfering with reindeer herding is also not allowed.

Abisko's Flora and Fauna

Abisko National Park is home to a diverse array of wildlife and plant life, set against a unique geological backdrop. Predators like bears, wolverines, and lynx roam the park, though they are elusive, leaving tracks visible in the winter snow. Moose are common in winter due to the relatively shallow snow, and reindeer can be spotted year-round. Birdlife is abundant, with golden eagles, white-tailed eagles, and other raptors soaring above, while various songbirds and wading birds inhabit the birch forests, alpine heaths, and marshlands.

The park's rich flore thrives in areas like Mount Njulla, known as "the flower mountain" for its diverse alpine plants, supported by limestone-rich soil and favorable sun exposure. Geologically, Abisko is defined by its mountain bedrock and dramatic landscapes shaped by ancient ice sheets, with the Abiskojakka river carving through the valley and forming steep canyon walls of schist and dolomite limestone.

What type of ecosystem(s) is found within this protected area?

Abisko features Artic and alpine ecosystems, including birch forests, alpine heaths, and wetlands.

Are there any species of note found in this protected area?

Abisko is home to species such as the artic fox (threatened in Sweden) and numerous birds, including the golden eagle and rare Artic warbler.
