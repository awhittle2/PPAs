Frequently Asked Questions



1. Where is Abisko National Park located?

Abisko National Park is in Norrbotten County, Sweden, near the Norwegian border, within the Artic Circle. It is part of the stunning Abiskodalen valley.


2. What activities can I do in Abisko National Park?

Popular activities include:

  • Viewing the northern lights (Aurora Borealis) in winter.
  • Experiencing the midnight sun in summer.
  • Hiking on the Kungsleden Trail.
  • Skiing, snowshoeing, and wildlife spotting.
  • Visiting the Aurora Sky Station for stunning views.


3. When is the best time to visit Abisko?

  • Winter (December to March): Ideal for northern lights and winter sports.
  • Summer (June to August): Perfect for hiking, experiencing the midnight sun, and enjoying lush green landscapes.


4. How big is Abisko National Park?

The park spans 77 square kilometers (30 square miles).


5. Is there an enterance fee for Abisko National Park?

No, enterance to Abisko National Park is free.


6. Can I camp in Abisko National Park?

Camping is allowed in designated areas, and there are nearby accommodations such as the Abisko Mountain Station.


7. Is Abisko accessible for visitors with limited mobility?

Some parts of the park and visitor centers offer accessibility features, but many trails are rugged and may not be suitable for all mobility levels.


8. What wildlife can I see in Abisko?

Abisko is home to a variety of wildlife, including:

  • Moose
  • Artic foxes
  • Reindeer
  • A wide range of birds, especially in summer.


9. When was Abisko National Park created?

The park was established in 1909.


10. Why was Abisko National Park created?

The park was created to preserve the unique Artic flora and fauna in its natural state and to support scientific research
