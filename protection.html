Protection of Abisko National Park



Management

Who manages this protected area?

The Norrbotten County Administrative Board oversees the management of the park, ensuring its conservation and operation.

Who funds the management of this protected area?

Funding comes from the Swedish government, allocated through the County Administrative Board and national conservation programs.

How is this protected area managed?

The Norrbotten County Administrative Board manages the park by enforcing conservation policies, maintaining infrastructure, and supportic scientific research.

How are specific issues managed?

  • Tourism: Sustainable tourism practices are promoted to minimize environmental impact.
  • Climate Change: The Abisko Scientific Research Station conducts research to monitor and address climate change effects on the park's ecosystems.
  • Cultural Preservation: Sami cultural sites are protected, and reindeer herding continues under traditional practices.

Policies and Challenges

What policies/laws/entities created this protected area?

The park was created under Sweden's first Nature Conservation Act of 1909, aimed at protecting natural areas and promoting scientific research.

What specific historical or cultural resources are protected?

The park protects Sami cultural sites, including ancient reindeer traps, hearths, and hut sites.

What are the main management issues or threats?

Climate change, which impacts species and ecosystems, is a major concern. Additionally, balancing tourism growth with conservation efforts presents ongoing challenges.
